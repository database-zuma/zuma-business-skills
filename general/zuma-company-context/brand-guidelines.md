# Zuma Brand Guidelines

**Source of Truth:** `Zuma Creative Hub Agents/ZUMA_BRAND BOOK_FINAL.pdf`

**Applies to:** Zuma Creative Hub Agents, Jarvis AI, and all Zuma work projects
**Does NOT apply to:** Personal projects (Youtube Channel AI, Career Development, etc.)

## Brand Tonality

### Witty
- Uses puns and wordplay (Zuma = "Cuma" meaning "just/only" in Indonesian)
- Universal humor that doesn't rely on specific cultural references
- Hopeful and optimistic outlook
- Makes complex technology approachable through clever language

### Casual
- Conversational, everyday language
- "Talk like a friend" - warm and approachable
- Avoid jargon and corporate-speak
- Use contractions and natural speech patterns

### Confident
- Empowering and encouraging
- Never intimidating or condescending
- Solution-focused and action-oriented
- Reassures users without being patronizing

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors
- **Zuma Teal:** `#002A3A`
  - Primary brand color
  - Use for headers, CTAs, key brand elements
  - Conveys trust, professionalism, depth

- **Zuma Green:** `#00E273`
  - Accent color for success states
  - Use sparingly for maximum impact
  - Highlights, active states, success messages
  - Conveys energy, growth, innovation

- **Plain White:** `#FFFFFF`
  - Primary background color
  - Creates generous whitespace
  - Allows content to breathe

#### Color Usage Guidelines
- **Use brand colors sparingly** for maximum impact
- Zuma Teal for primary actions and brand moments
- Zuma Green for highlights and success feedback
- Maintain generous whitespace with white backgrounds
- Never dilute brand colors - use at full saturation

### Aesthetic Direction

#### Japandi Minimalism
A fusion of Japanese minimalism and Scandinavian warmth:
- **Rustic minimalism** with generous whitespace
- **Clean lines** and defined borders
- **Functional beauty** - every element has purpose
- **Natural materials** feel (even in digital interfaces)

#### Lighting & Shadows
- **Strong, bright sunlight** with high contrast
- **Deep, dramatic shadows** for depth
- **Crisp borders** with defined shadows (not blurred or soft)
- **Hard light** rather than diffused
- Creates a tropical, sun-drenched atmosphere

#### Layout Principles
- **Generous whitespace** - don't fear empty space
- **Grid-based layouts** for structure
- **Asymmetric balance** for visual interest
- **Clear hierarchy** through size and spacing
- **Breathing room** around all elements

### Typography

#### Primary Font: GT Walsheim
- Modern, clean sans-serif
- Use for headings, UI elements, body text
- Geometric construction with warmth
- Available in multiple weights

#### Secondary Font: BDO Grotesk
- Alternative sans-serif option
- Use for specific design moments
- Slightly more unique character

#### Type Scale Guidelines
- **Headlines:** Bold, generous spacing
- **Body text:** Regular weight, readable line height (1.5-1.7)
- **UI elements:** Medium weight for clarity
- **Captions:** Lighter weight, smaller size

## Application Guidelines

### When Designing Zuma Projects

#### UI/UX
1. **Start with whitespace** - design around breathing room
2. **Use brand colors sparingly** - let white dominate
3. **Apply crisp shadows** to create depth
4. **Maintain clean borders** - no blur effects
5. **Follow Japandi principles** - minimal, functional, warm

#### Copy & Content
1. **Write conversationally** - like talking to a friend
2. **Use wordplay thoughtfully** - witty, not forced
3. **Be encouraging** - empower users to take action
4. **Avoid jargon** - keep it simple and clear
5. **Stay optimistic** - solution-focused tone

#### Component Design
- **Buttons:** Clean rectangles with subtle shadows
- **Cards:** Generous padding, crisp borders, defined shadows
- **Forms:** Spacious fields, clear labels, encouraging feedback
- **Navigation:** Minimal, clear hierarchy
- **Modals:** Centered, ample whitespace, easy to dismiss

### Brand Moments

Use full brand treatment (Zuma Teal + Zuma Green + brand tonality) for:
- **Landing pages** and marketing materials
- **Onboarding flows** and first-time experiences
- **Key feature announcements**
- **Success states** and celebrations
- **Brand touchpoints** (emails, notifications)

Use lighter brand presence for:
- **Daily workflow interfaces** (focus on functionality)
- **Dense data displays** (prioritize readability)
- **Technical documentation** (clarity over personality)

## Examples in Practice

### Good Examples
- **Header:** Zuma Teal background, white text, generous padding
- **CTA Button:** Zuma Green with crisp shadow, confident copy like "Let's go"
- **Success Message:** Zuma Green accent, witty copy like "Cuma perfect!"
- **Card Layout:** White background, defined border, dramatic shadow, ample padding
- **Empty State:** Generous whitespace, encouraging message, clear next action

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Soft, blurred shadows (use crisp, defined shadows)
- ❌ Overuse of brand colors (maintain whitespace dominance)
- ❌ Cluttered layouts (embrace generous spacing)
- ❌ Corporate jargon (keep it conversational)
- ❌ Intimidating or complex language (stay approachable)

## Cross-Project Consistency

### Shared Brand Elements
- **Logo treatment** - consistent across all Zuma projects
- **Color usage** - same Teal + Green + White palette
- **Typography** - GT Walsheim or BDO Grotesk
- **Shadow style** - crisp, defined, dramatic
- **Spacing system** - consistent whitespace scale
- **Copy tone** - witty, casual, confident

### Project-Specific Adaptation
While maintaining brand consistency:
- **Jarvis AI:** More conversational, assistant-focused
- **Creative Hub Agents:** More creative, expressive, visual
- **Data Analytics:** More functional, clarity-focused

## Quick Decision Guide

**When choosing colors:**
→ Default to white background, use Teal/Green sparingly

**When writing copy:**
→ Read it aloud - does it sound like talking to a friend?

**When designing layout:**
→ Add more whitespace than you think you need

**When adding shadows:**
→ Make them crisp and dramatic, not soft

**When in doubt:**
→ Refer to the Brand Book PDF for final decisions
