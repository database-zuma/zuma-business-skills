# Insight Hierarchy Checklist

**Purpose:** Ensure analysis progresses from descriptive → diagnostic → predictive → prescriptive.

**Goal:** Every analysis should reach Level 4 (Prescriptive).

---

## Level 1: Descriptive (What happened?)

**Characteristics:**
- Data reporting
- Charts, tables, metrics
- Historical facts
- No interpretation

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What are the key metrics?
- [ ] What changed vs last period?
- [ ] What's the current state?
- [ ] What patterns exist in the data?

**Example:**
"Top 20 SKU generated Rp 2.9B revenue YTD 2025. Average YoY change is -83%."

**Quality bar:** ✅ Necessary foundation, but NOT sufficient alone.

---

## Level 2: Diagnostic (Why did it happen?)

**Characteristics:**
- Root cause analysis
- Pattern identification
- Correlation/causation
- Explains mechanisms

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Why did this metric change?
- [ ] What caused the pattern?
- [ ] What are the underlying drivers?
- [ ] Which factors are most influential?

**Example:**
"YoY decline caused by:
- Product lifecycle maturity (2-3 year old designs)
- Market saturation (same SKU flooding market)
- Intensified competition (local brands launching quarterly)
- Possible cannibalization from new SKU"

**Quality bar:** ✅ Essential for credibility. Don't guess — investigate.

---

## Level 3: Predictive (What will happen?)

**Characteristics:**
- Forecasting
- Scenario modeling
- Trend projection
- Risk assessment

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What will happen if we don't act?
- [ ] What are possible future scenarios?
- [ ] What's the best/base/worst case?
- [ ] When will we see impact?

**Example:**
"Forecast scenarios through Q4 2025:
- Best case (-50% YoY): Lose Rp 1.5B revenue
- Base case (-80% YoY): Lose Rp 2.3B revenue ← Most likely
- Worst case (-95% YoY): Lose Rp 2.7B, some SKU die completely"

**Quality bar:** ✅ Creates urgency. Shows stakes.

---

## Level 4: Prescriptive (What should we do?)

**Characteristics:**
- Actionable recommendations
- Clear next steps
- Resource requirements
- Expected ROI

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What specific actions should we take?
- [ ] What's the expected impact?
- [ ] What resources are needed?
- [ ] When should we start?
- [ ] How will we measure success?

**Example:**
"Recommendation: Launch 10-15 new SKU per quarter
- When: Q2 2025 (April-June)
- Investment: Rp 150M (design + production)
- Expected impact: Recover Rp 500M revenue by Q3
- ROI: 3.3x within 6 months
- Risk mitigation: Pilot 3 SKU first
- Success metric: 30% of new SKU contribute >Rp 30M each"

**Quality bar:** ✅ THIS is the goal. Must be SMART + quantified.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

### Step 1: Identify Current Level

Review your analysis. Which level have you reached?

- **Only Level 1?** You have a data report, not an analysis
- **Stopped at Level 2?** You explained the problem but didn't solve it
- **Reached Level 3?** Good urgency, but no action
- **Made it to Level 4?** ✅ Complete analysis

### Step 2: Fill the Gaps

For each level not yet reached:

1. Write down the questions from that level
2. Answer them with data/evidence
3. Iterate until you reach Level 4

### Step 3: Validate Quality

**Level 1 (Descriptive):**
- [ ] Metrics are accurate
- [ ] Data sources clear
- [ ] Time periods consistent

**Level 2 (Diagnostic):**
- [ ] Root causes identified (not just symptoms)
- [ ] Backed by evidence (not assumptions)
- [ ] Multiple factors considered

**Level 3 (Predictive):**
- [ ] Scenarios are realistic
- [ ] Timeframe specified
- [ ] Assumptions stated

**Level 4 (Prescriptive):**
- [ ] Recommendations are SMART
- [ ] ROI quantified
- [ ] Resources specified
- [ ] Risks addressed

---

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Stopping at Level 1
**Problem:** "Here's the data. Discuss."  
**Fix:** Always push to Level 4 (action)

### Mistake #2: Skipping Level 2
**Problem:** Jump from "revenue down" to "launch products" without WHY  
**Fix:** Diagnose root cause first

### Mistake #3: No Forecast (Level 3)
**Problem:** Can't create urgency without showing future impact  
**Fix:** Model scenarios (best/base/worst)

### Mistake #4: Generic Prescriptions
**Problem:** "Improve performance", "Increase sales"  
**Fix:** Specific, quantified actions with ROI

---

## Example Progression

**Level 1 (Descriptive):**
"Bali branch has Rp 27.6B revenue from 18 stores."

**Level 2 (Diagnostic):**
"WHY is Bali performing? Tourism traffic 2-3x higher than urban malls. Average spend per customer Rp 300K vs Rp 180K Jakarta. Stores located near beaches/attractions."

**Level 3 (Predictive):**
"IF we replicate Bali model to 5 more tourism areas (Canggu, Seminyak, Labuan Bajo x2, Raja Ampat), we project Rp 7.5B additional revenue/year (5 stores x Rp 1.5B avg)."

**Level 4 (Prescriptive):**
"RECOMMENDATION: Open 5 stores in tourism hotspots by Q3-Q4 2025.
- Investment: Rp 1B (Rp 200M per store)
- Timeline: 2-3 stores Q3, remaining Q4
- Expected revenue: Rp 7.5B/year
- Payback: 14-16 months per store
- Risk mitigation: Start with Canggu/Seminyak (proven), then expand
- Owner: Retail expansion team
- Success metric: Avg revenue Rp 1.5B per store within 12 months"

## Self-Assessment

Rate your current analysis:

- **1 star:** Descriptive only (data dump)
- **2 stars:** Diagnostic reached (explained why)
- **3 stars:** Predictive added (forecast scenarios)
- **4 stars:** Prescriptive complete (actionable recs with ROI)

**Target:** Always aim for 4 stars.
