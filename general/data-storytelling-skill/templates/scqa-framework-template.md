# SCQA Framework Template

**SCQA = Situation → Complication → Question → Answer**

McKinsey's classic storytelling structure for business presentations.

---

## Component 1: Situation (Set the Context)

**Purpose:** Establish common ground. Where are we today?

**Elements:**
- Current state
- Baseline metrics
- What's normal/expected
- Uncontroversial facts

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What's the current situation?
- [ ] What context does audience need?
- [ ] What's the starting point?

**Template:**
```
[Company/Product] has [current state metrics]. 
Over the past [timeframe], we've [performance summary]. 
Our position is [market context].
```

**Example:**
```
Zuma Indonesia operates 598 SKU across 6 branches 
(Jatim, Jakarta, Bali, Lombok, Sulawesi, Batam) 
with YTD 2025 revenue of Rp 50.4B. 
Our top 20 SKU historically contribute 35% of total revenue.
```

**Tone:** Neutral, factual, non-alarming.

---

## Component 2: Complication (Introduce Tension)

**Purpose:** Create urgency. What's changed? What's at risk?

**Elements:**
- Problem discovered
- Unexpected change
- Risk/threat
- Opportunity cost

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What's wrong or at stake?
- [ ] Why should audience care NOW?
- [ ] What's the trigger for action?

**Template:**
```
However, [unexpected problem/change]. 
This means [negative consequence]. 
If we don't act, [forecast downside].
```

**Example:**
```
However, 19 out of 20 top SKU are declining -70% to -90% year-over-year. 
This means our core revenue engine is collapsing. 
If we don't act, we face a Rp 2.3B revenue loss by Q4 2025.
```

**Tone:** Urgent, concerning, specific.

---

## Component 3: Question (Frame the Decision)

**Purpose:** Guide the narrative. What question needs answering?

**Elements:**
- Explicit or implicit
- Decision-focused
- Forward-looking

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What decision must be made?
- [ ] What options exist?
- [ ] What's the key question?

**Template (Explicit):**
```
The critical question is: [decision question]?
```

**Template (Implicit):**
[Skip explicit question, let Answer provide it implicitly]

**Example (Explicit):**
```
The critical question is: How do we reverse the decline 
and return to sustainable growth?
```

**Example (Implicit):**
[Move straight to Answer — question is implied: "What should we do?"]

**Note:** Implicit often works better (less wordy). Use explicit when question is non-obvious.

---

## Component 4: Answer (Present the Recommendation)

**Purpose:** Provide the solution. Clear, specific, actionable.

**Elements:**
- Main recommendation
- Supporting rationale
- Expected outcome
- Implementation approach

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] What should we do?
- [ ] Why this solution?
- [ ] What's the expected impact?

**Template:**
```
We recommend [main action]. 
This addresses [root cause] by [mechanism]. 
Expected impact: [quantified outcome] within [timeframe].
Implementation: [high-level approach].
```

**Example:**
```
We recommend a 3-Play Strategy to reverse decline:

Play #1: Product Refresh — Launch 10-15 new SKU per quarter, 
focused on Men's & Baby categories. This directly addresses 
product lifecycle fatigue. Investment: Rp 150M. 
Expected recovery: Rp 500M by Q3, ROI 3.3x.

Play #2: Geographic Expansion — Open 5 stores in proven 
tourism hotspots (Canggu, Seminyak, Labuan Bajo x2, Raja Ampat). 
Capitalizes on tourism model that's 2-3x more productive than urban. 
Investment: Rp 1B. Expected revenue: Rp 7.5B/year, 14-month payback.

Play #3: Digital Acceleration — Scale online channel 50% YoY 
via SEO/SEM investment and marketplace optimization. 
Leverages highest-ROI channel (Rp 11.4B per channel). 
Investment: Rp 200M/year. Incremental revenue: Rp 11B.

Combined Impact: Rp 19B additional revenue, 
Rp 1.4B investment, 13.6x blended ROI.
```

**Tone:** Confident, specific, action-oriented.

---

## How to Build Your SCQA

### Step 1: Start with Answer

**Counter-intuitive but effective:**
1. Write the Answer first (your recommendation)
2. Then work backwards to Situation/Complication
3. This ensures the story supports your conclusion

### Step 2: Craft the Complication

**This is the heart of the story:**
- Make it urgent (timeline, quantified risk)
- Make it specific (not vague "declining performance")
- Create tension (but don't exaggerate)

### Step 3: Establish Situation

**Just enough context:**
- Don't over-explain baseline
- 2-3 sentences max
- Uncontroversial facts

### Step 4: Decide on Question

**Explicit or implicit?**
- Implicit works 80% of the time (cleaner flow)
- Explicit when decision isn't obvious

## SCQA Variants

### Variant 1: Opportunity-Led

**Use when:** You're presenting an opportunity (not a problem)

**Structure:**
- **Situation:** Current state (stable/normal)
- **Complication:** Opportunity discovered ("we found untapped potential")
- **Question:** How do we capture it?
- **Answer:** Strategy to seize opportunity

**Example:**
```
S: Zuma has 20 stores generating Rp 50B annually
C: We discovered tourism stores generate 2-3x revenue vs urban (Bali/Lombok avg Rp 1.5-2.7B per store)
Q: How do we capitalize on this model?
A: Open 5 new stores in tourism areas → Rp 7.5B upside
```

### Variant 2: Choice-Led

**Use when:** Multiple options exist, need to decide

**Structure:**
- **Situation:** Current state
- **Complication:** Decision point (not necessarily a problem)
- **Question:** Which option should we choose?
- **Answer:** Recommended option + why

### Variant 3: Change-Led

**Use when:** External change forces action

**Structure:**
- **Situation:** How things were
- **Complication:** External change (market shift, regulation, competitor move)
- **Question:** How do we adapt?
- **Answer:** Adaptation strategy

---

## Checklist

Before finalizing SCQA narrative:

- [ ] Situation is brief (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Situation establishes baseline (uncontroversial)
- [ ] Complication creates urgency (specific, quantified)
- [ ] Complication shows why NOW matters
- [ ] Question (if explicit) is decision-focused
- [ ] Answer is specific and actionable
- [ ] Answer addresses the Complication's root cause
- [ ] Expected impact is quantified
- [ ] Timeline/next steps clear

---

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Too Much Situation
**Problem:** 10 slides of background before getting to problem  
**Fix:** 1 slide max for Situation

### Mistake #2: Weak Complication
**Problem:** "Sales are declining" (vague, not urgent)  
**Fix:** "19 of 20 SKU declining -80% YoY, Rp 2.3B at risk by Q4"

### Mistake #3: No Question
**Problem:** Jump from Complication → Answer without framing decision  
**Fix:** Usually fine (implicit works), but ensure Answer clearly addresses Complication

### Mistake #4: Generic Answer
**Problem:** "We should improve performance"  
**Fix:** Specific actions, quantified impact, timeline
