# Data Storytelling Skill

**Purpose:** Transform descriptive data reports into compelling strategic narratives with deep insights and actionable recommendations.

**Use when:** Creating business presentations, performance analyses, strategic recommendations, executive reports.

---

## Core Principle

**Answer First, Then Support**

❌ **Bad:** "Here's data → here's more data → conclusion"  
✅ **Good:** "Conclusion → why it matters → supporting evidence → action plan"

---

## Framework 1: SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer)

McKinsey's classic structure for business storytelling.

### Components

**1. Situation** — Set the context (current state)
- Where are we today?
- What's the baseline?
- Establish common ground

**2. Complication** — Introduce tension (problem/opportunity)
- What's changed or at risk?
- Why should audience care?
- Create urgency

**3. Question** — Frame the decision
- What should we do?
- Explicit or implicit
- Guides the narrative

**4. Answer** — Present the recommendation
- Clear, specific action
- Supported by data
- Quantified impact

### Example Application

**Situation:** "Zuma has 598 SKU across 6 branches with YTD revenue Rp 50.4B"

**Complication:** "BUT 19 of 20 top SKU declining -70% to -90% YoY — Rp 2.3B revenue at risk by Q4"

**Question:** "How do we reverse decline and drive growth?"

**Answer:** "3-Play Strategy: Product refresh (10-15 new SKU/quarter) + Geographic expansion (5 tourism stores) + Digital acceleration (50% YoY growth) → Rp 19B upside"

## Framework 2: Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto)

**Structure:**
```
Main Conclusion (top of pyramid)
    ├── Supporting Argument 1
    │   ├── Evidence 1a
    │   └── Evidence 1b
    ├── Supporting Argument 2
    │   ├── Evidence 2a
    │   └── Evidence 2b
    └── Supporting Argument 3
        ├── Evidence 3a
        └── Evidence 3b
```

**Application Rules:**
1. Lead with the answer (not the data)
2. Group ideas into logical themes (3-4 max)
3. Each group has one main message
4. Support with data/evidence
5. Each slide = one key insight

**Slide Headlines:**
- ❌ "Top 20 SKU Performance" (topic)
- ✅ "Core revenue collapsing — 19 of 20 top SKU declining -80% YoY" (insight)

## Framework 3: Insight Hierarchy (Gartner Model)

Progress through levels of analysis depth:

### Level 1: Descriptive (What happened?)
- Data reporting
- Charts, tables, metrics
- **Example:** "Top 20 SKU generated Rp 2.9B revenue"

### Level 2: Diagnostic (Why did it happen?)
- Root cause analysis
- Pattern identification
- **Example:** "YoY decline caused by product lifecycle maturity + market saturation + intensified competition"

### Level 3: Predictive (What will happen?)
- Forecasting
- Scenario modeling
- **Example:** "If unchanged, base case scenario: lose Rp 2.3B revenue by Q4 2025"

### Level 4: Prescriptive (What should we do?)
- Recommendations
- Action plans with ROI
- **Example:** "Launch 10-15 new SKU per quarter, invest Rp 150M, recover Rp 500M by Q3, ROI 3.3x"

**Quality Bar:** Every analysis should reach Level 4 (Prescriptive).

---

## Framework 4: Narrative Arc for Business

Classic story structure applied to data presentations:

**1. Setup** — Where we are today  
**2. Conflict** — Problem/opportunity/challenge  
**3. Journey** — What we discovered in the data  
**4. Resolution** — What we should do  
**5. Call to Action** — Next steps with urgency

**Tension is key:** Without conflict/stakes, presentation is boring.

## Best Practices

### Slide Structure (McKinsey Style)

**Every slide must have:**

1. **Headline** — The insight/conclusion (not the topic)
   - Should be a complete sentence
   - Tells the "so what?"
   
2. **Supporting Visual** — Chart/table/diagram
   - Annotate with interpretation
   - Remove clutter
   - Focus attention on key data points
   
3. **So What Box** (optional) — 1-2 sentence implication
   - "This means..."
   - "Therefore we should..."

### Actionable Recommendations (SMART)

Every recommendation must include:

- **Specific:** Not "improve products" but "launch 10-15 new SKU in Men's & Baby"
- **Measurable:** "Recover Rp 500M revenue by Q3"
- **Achievable:** Within resource constraints
- **Relevant:** Tied to root cause
- **Time-bound:** "By end of Q2 2025"

**Plus:**
- **Investment needed:** Rp 150M for design + production
- **Expected ROI:** 3.3x within 6 months
- **Risk mitigation:** Pilot 3 SKU first before full rollout
- **Owner:** Who executes (Product team)

### Quantify Everything

**Weak recommendations:**
- "Expand to tourism areas"
- "Launch new products"
- "Improve online channel"

**Strong recommendations:**
- "Open 5 stores in tourism hotspots (Canggu, Seminyak, LBJ x2, Raja Ampat) by Q3-Q4"
- "Launch 10-15 new SKU per quarter starting Q2, focus Men's & Baby"
- "Grow online 50% YoY (Rp 22.9B → Rp 34B) via Rp 200M marketing investment"

### Include Forecasts & Scenarios

Don't just describe current state — project forward:

**Scenario Planning:**
- **Best case:** What if everything goes right?
- **Base case:** Most likely outcome (use this for recommendations)
- **Worst case:** What if things go wrong?

**Example:**
- Best case (-50% YoY): Lose Rp 1.5B
- Base case (-80% YoY): Lose Rp 2.3B ← Plan around this
- Worst case (-95% YoY): Lose Rp 2.7B, SKU die

### Annotate Visuals

Charts should guide the eye:

- **Highlight key data:** Use color to focus attention
- **Add labels:** "This is the problem", "This is the opportunity"
- **Show trendlines:** Not just points
- **Compare:** YoY, benchmark, target

## Presentation Structure Template

### Opening (2-3 slides)

**Slide 1: Title**
- Provocative, not generic
- ❌ "Performance Analysis Q1 2025"
- ✅ "Zuma at a Crossroads: Pivot or Decline"

**Slide 2: Executive Summary**
- One-slide answer to "so what?"
- Challenge + Opportunity + Recommendation
- Quantified (revenue at risk, upside, investment needed)

**Slide 3: Roadmap** (optional)
- What we'll cover
- Sets expectations

### Body (4-6 slides)

Organize by themes (not data types):

**Theme 1: The Challenge**
- What's wrong/at risk
- WHY analysis (root cause)
- Forecast impact (what if we don't act)

**Theme 2: The Opportunity**
- What's working/untapped
- Pattern discovery
- Proof points

**Theme 3: The Strategy**
- Recommendations (3-5 plays)
- Each play: What/Why/How/Impact/Investment/Timeline
- Combined ROI

**Theme 4: Implementation**
- Roadmap (quarterly milestones)
- Resource requirements
- Success metrics
- Risk mitigation

### Closing (1-2 slides)

**Slide N-1: Summary**
- Recap key findings
- Reinforce recommendations

**Slide N: Call to Action**
- Specific next steps
- Ownership
- Deadline
- Urgency statement

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. "Data Dump" Syndrome
**Problem:** Showing all data because you collected it  
**Fix:** Show only data that supports the story

### 2. Buried Lede
**Problem:** Conclusion at the end (after 30 slides of data)  
**Fix:** Answer first (Pyramid Principle), then support

### 3. No "So What"
**Problem:** Descriptive insights without implication  
**Fix:** Every insight needs action/implication

### 4. Generic Recommendations
**Problem:** "Improve performance", "Launch products"  
**Fix:** SMART criteria + quantified impact

### 5. Missing "Why"
**Problem:** Reporting what happened, not why  
**Fix:** Diagnostic analysis (root cause)

### 6. No Urgency
**Problem:** Recommendations feel optional  
**Fix:** Forecast downside, set deadlines, create tension

### 7. Too Many Slides
**Problem:** 50+ slides, audience loses thread  
**Fix:** Aim for 8-12 slides (McKinsey deck length)

---

## Checklist: Is Your Analysis "JOSS"?

Before presenting, verify:

- [ ] **Executive summary slide** exists (answer first)
- [ ] **Headlines are insights** (not topics)
- [ ] **Every slide has "so what?"** box or implication
- [ ] **Root cause identified** (diagnostic, not just descriptive)
- [ ] **Forecast included** ("what if we don't act?")
- [ ] **Recommendations are SMART** (specific, measurable, time-bound)
- [ ] **ROI quantified** for each recommendation
- [ ] **Investment/resources** specified
- [ ] **Risk mitigation** addressed
- [ ] **Success metrics** defined
- [ ] **Timeline/roadmap** clear
- [ ] **Call to action** with deadline
- [ ] **Narrative arc** feels compelling (setup → conflict → resolution)
- [ ] **Visuals annotated** (not just raw charts)
- [ ] **Data supports story** (not vice versa)

## When to Use This Skill

**Always use for:**
- Strategic business presentations
- Executive reports
- Performance analyses
- Investment proposals
- Board decks

**Especially when:**
- Presenting to senior leadership
- Requesting budget/resources
- Driving decision-making
- Complex multi-faceted problems

**Goal:** Transform data → decisions. Not just inform, but convince and mobilize action.
